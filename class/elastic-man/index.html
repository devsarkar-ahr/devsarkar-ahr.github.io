Elastic Man

Elastic Man is a browser-based casual game focused on physics-driven interaction and freeform manipulation. The game places a highly elastic face on screen and allows the player to stretch, pull, twist, and distort it using direct mouse or touch input. There are no levels, scores, or progression systems to manage. Instead, the experience centers on experimentation and immediate visual feedback. Every movement reacts in real time, with exaggerated physics that amplify even small inputs. The goal is not to win but to explore how the elastic model responds to different motions and speeds. Players control the experience entirely through dragging and holding actions, which makes the game accessible without tutorials or prior experience. Elastic Man keeps engagement through its unpredictable deformations and humorous reactions, similar in spirit to simple interaction-based titles like Arrow Master. The game runs directly in the browser and loads quickly, making it suitable for short, distraction-free play sessions.

Developer / Release Date

Developed by: David Li
Release Date: 2020

Controls

PC Controls:

  • Mouse Left Click (Hold) = Grab face or body

  • Mouse Drag = Stretch and move the elastic model

Mobile Controls:

  • Tap and Hold = Grab face or body

  • Drag Finger = Stretch and move the elastic model

How to Play

Elastic Man starts instantly with the character displayed at the center of the screen. The player presses and holds the mouse button or touches the screen to grab any part of the face or head. Dragging in any direction stretches the model, and releasing returns it to its resting shape. Faster movements create more extreme distortions, while slow drags produce smoother elastic motion. There is no failure state, time limit, or objective to complete. The player controls the pace entirely by choosing how aggressively or gently to interact. Repeated pulls on different areas create varied reactions, which keeps the experience engaging despite its simplicity. Because the game has no progression system, players can stop and resume at any time without losing progress. The focus remains on tactile interaction, physics response, and visual humor rather than challenge or mastery.
